Interests in IT

Computers have always amused me. I am quite interested in learning computer languages and coding them to create a software or for other uses in security field. I developed my interest in coding during the last few years of my secondary education, watching my elder brother make games and talk about how he uses coding for analysing and implementing business strategies for the company he worked. I was very keen about the topic, therefore I used to carry out some researches, after which, today at present, I am quite thrilled about the ideas and opportunities that learning coding will provide me.

For pusuing my interests in computer programming, I needed an institute which can inbuilt the proper practical skills required in the real world.The university is highly reagrded and ranked in study and student employment satisfaction rate.Unlike most of the institutes that focus on theoretical ,and sometimes, useless concepts, RMIT university is the one that makes it's students trained in what the world needs. IT is a subject of practicality, which means implying your ideas and skills so as to see what change can it make in the real world. RMIT, not only makes student independent in expressing and applying their thoughts, but also provides them some industry experience for working further. Overall, RMIT focuses in digging the skills deep into the minds of it's students rather than creating a deceptive layer of cramming.

For such a highly recognised university, I expect to develop all the essential skills required for entering the workforce or working independently. I expect to grab as many computer languages under my belt as I can along with deep understanding of computers' world for implementing my learned skills in any field I desire.
