Ideal Job

Furthermore, time management and multiple task management skills are also crucial.Updating and figuring out the right technology for the right purpose is an essential aspect of undertaking such a job.In short, everything involved in multiplying the sales of the company will be undertaken by the business analyst.
As currently I'm studying Bachelor of IT in RMIT, I donot have any previous experience or skills in the subject except for working efficiently in Microsoft Word and Excel.I came to RMIT straight after my high school,where I mainly studied three subjects- Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics. So, for grabbing all the skills required to get into the IT workforce, I'm here in RMIT, pursuing my degree in IT.
With the skills and qualification that I have now, I'll not be able to apply for my ideal job because I need to accomplish something before getting into such a job.Therefore, in addition to studying at RMIT, I will try to gain some experience in software development and analytics by working on paid or unpaid internships in companies that require minimal or no experience but just the knowledge on how to implement the skills during my breaks.Also, RMIT gives it's students an opportunity to select major stream of study along with some minor coureses. In that case, i will be opting for software development and software testing along with organisational analysis, logistics and management that will help giving me a further overview on how my ideal job would be handled appropriately. After the completion of my course, I will aim to be working as a professional in the field of IT to gain experience of around 3-4 years along with doing some independent researchs and experiments to enhance my skills.After all these years of studying and working as an IT professional in a certain company,wherein I would also have to polish on my leadership qualities, I will be eligible to acheive my ideal job.
